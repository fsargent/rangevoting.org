Honeybees use range voting – executive summary

  • Scientists analysing video footage have shown that Range Voting has been used in hundreds of trillions of elections held by social insects (honeybees and ants) to select nest locations.
  • The bees' decision-making process can be interpreted almost exactly as "range voting with intentional blanks," albeit implemented with a rather clever and nonobvious algorithm.
  • That's far more democracy experience that humanity has ever had (also more than computers can simulate).
  • Biologists regard ants as the most successful land animal ever and the evidence from entomological observations and computer simulations is that honeybee elections select the objectively best candidate ≈3 times more often (90% versus 32%) than human elections (with comparable numbers of candidates and voters) would.
  • That's mainly because of the flaws in the "plurality voting system" currently most prevalent among humans.
